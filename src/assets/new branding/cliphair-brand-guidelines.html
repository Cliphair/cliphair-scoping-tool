Brand Descriptor

The simplest, most complete description of what Cliphair is — inclusive of all key brand attributes and exclusive to Cliphair.

CLIPHAIR provide the most complete range of 100% premium-grade human hair extensions and extension hair-care products, tools and accessories on the UK market. Products are sold principally online. Consumers report the product as good value for money, and two tiers of product quality are offered. CLIPHAIR offer the best customer service in the industry.

Copy Approach

The copy approach is not the copy itself. It defines the direction and vibe.

Core Rule

Never take the brand too seriously — but always take solving the customer's problem seriously.

Copy should make it clear that Cliphair has the solution, while remaining approachable, sometimes funny or playful.

Voice Model

Best friend or hair stylist

Provide the best advice in the way you might want your best friend or hair stylist to give it — warm, honest, helpful, with zero condescension.

Brand Metaphor

"It's not just about adding hair — it's about BLOSSOMING into your fullest self."

The brand metaphor is a flower in bloom. The hair is already full of potential — Cliphair helps it open up and flourish. It's not about changing someone; it's about giving them confidence to fully blossom.

🌱 Before — The Bud

The customer at the beginning of their journey. A sense of limitation, concealment, or restraint — like a bud before bloom.

🌸 After — The Full Bloom

The full customer transformation. Confidence, radiance, and full self-expression — a bloom at its peak.

Flower Aesthetic Direction

Not just any flower. Look for blooms that feel almost architectural — strong structure, full body, and that "damn, that's perfect" finish. The dahlia is the primary reference: bold, full, and beautifully built.

Not this

Scent, ingredient, or being "pretty"

This

Structure, precision, quality — perfectly crafted beauty

The goal

Precise, dense, layered, full. Symmetrical, balanced, three-dimensional shape.

Logo

Logo Origins

The symbol is built from four rotating letter C's coming together to form a bloom — capturing the Cliphair transformation in a single mark. The full logo comprises two parts: the symbol (the flower/marque) and the Cliphair wordmark.

Logo Configurations

Ranged Layout

Best for most layouts and everyday use. Works well in headers, digital formats, and horizontal layouts.

Centred Layout

Ideal for narrow, stacked, or centred layouts. Great for social posts, small spaces, or vertical formats.

Strapline Usage

Strapline: "Be the you you want to be." — Optional. Use mainly in marketing or promotional pieces (ads, campaign assets). Not for everyday informational materials like leaflets or stationery.

Micro Logo

A special micro version exists for very small spaces. Only use the micro logo when width drops below 90px or 15mm. Anything larger — use the standard logo.

Logo Colour

Primary Logo Colour

Charcoal — Default

Pantone: BLACK 7C | Print: C65 M60 Y65 K65 | Screen: R59 G56 B57 | Hex: #3B3838

Can also appear in off-white when the background calls for it. Avoid using logo on very light yellow or deep purple backgrounds — insufficient contrast.

Independent Components Rule

⚠️ Non-negotiable: Both the symbol and the wordmark must appear somewhere within the same piece of collateral. They don't have to be adjacent, but they must both be present. Think of them like best friends — they don't have to stand next to each other, but they have to show up to the same event.

Marque

The logo marque is one of the most fun and flexible brand assets. It's distinctive, recognisable, and built to work in many different ways across platforms.

  • Use it on its own, layer it into layouts, or let it interact with the product (e.g. wrapping around a lock of hair)
  • Experiment freely — as long as it stays clear and on-brand
  • Can be used at large scale as a dominant compositional element

Panelling

The outer frame of the marque can be used as a panel or window device.

Use for

Framing photography

Use for

Showcasing textures or colour swatches

Use for

Highlighting important text, quotes, or instructions

Also works as a die-cut element — giving layouts a stylish, branded touch. Perfect as a device to highlight key information, house a quote, or an instruction.

10

Motif

The marque rotated to form a circular pattern with a floral vibe — a more complex, repeating design element.

  • Can sit alongside model or product photography
  • Can create stand-out panels and feature areas
  • Place behind the model — three quarters of the central marque within the motif should be visible
  • Layouts should be kept simple when using the motif — it is more complex than other toolkit components and needs room to breathe
11

Glass Marque

A semi-transparent, sleek version of the marque that adds extra dimension without hiding what's underneath. Gives a layered, fresh, crisp look.

  • Use sparingly — a little goes a long way
  • Never use alongside the solid marque in the same space — one per area
  • When positioned over text, leave approximately 50% of the glass marque clear without text beneath it
  • Keep arrangements simple — over-complication adds noise and overpowers the glass marque

Typography Hierarchy

Typography centres around large headline placement, which typically dictates the layout of a page.

Hierarchy Levels

  • Headline: Large, bold, expressive — Chocolatier Bold. Short copy only.
  • Second Level / Intro: Relatively large — communicates confidence. Engaging paragraph or quote.
  • Body Copy: Regular weight. Bold for subheadings. General text panels.
  • Captions / Notes: Smallest text on the page. Regular weight. Annotations only.

Style Principles

  • Headlines should be short and expressive
  • Large placement creates confidence and dominates the layout
  • Create cascading levels of importance on the page
  • Never use primary vibrant colours for typography
  • Typography colours: Charcoal (#3B3838) or Off-White (#FFF2E0)

Quality Seal

Seal reads: "100% HUMAN · QUALITY HAIR ASSURANCE" with the marque at centre.

  • Represents high standards, rigorous QC, and 100% real human hair
  • Include at least once on a piece of collateral (e.g. a brochure)
  • Use sparingly on promotional materials — one seal per piece is plenty
  • Looks excellent as an emboss — adds a subtle touch of luxury
17

Pattern

A geometric wavy pattern that mimics hair strands.

  • Can be used in any brand colour depending on desired prominence
  • Preferred use: coloured white, layered over one of the paler colours — a good balance between punchy and insipid
  • Use as decoration (e.g. wrapping paper) or to complement more prominent graphics
  • Should never overpower primary graphics — it is a supporting element

People Imagery

Photography Direction

Distinct in a sea of sameness within the hair extension category. Photography should project a blend of character, playfulness, and irreverent tone. Models should seem comfortable and confident in their own skin, not taking themselves too seriously, embracing fun.

Colour

Images should have vibrancy through the primary vibrants colour palette — visually striking and recognisable with a strong focus on the product.

Flower Prop Rule

  • A single flower should appear in a spirited and light-hearted way
  • Allow models to express themselves using the flower in different poses
  • Aim for a 60:40 ratio of flower inclusion to none
  • Not every shot needs a prop — look for poses that lend themselves to its inclusion
  • Keep shots fresh and varied across the portfolio

Flower in Shot — Four Approaches

Expressive prop

Used playfully — the flower takes an active, expressive role in the shot

Face frame

Flower in front of the face, becoming a more prominent component of the shot

Subtle complement

Not centre stage, but complementing the composition

Character enhancer

Used to enhance the fun character of the shot — models play with the flower

Expression Guide

Pursing lips, hair falling over face — fun, playful character

Playful poses in wider shots, playing with the hair

Interact and play with the hair — don't be scared

Cheeky gestures suggesting a stolen, fun-loving moment

Self-deprecating and humorous expressions — warm, welcoming, approachable

Simpler, natural movement shots — balance the super expressive ones

Models with unique or stand-out features — makes Cliphair feel different

Emphasise volume and bounce through hair movement

Candid, thoughtful moments — always positive in feeling

Hair Quality in Imagery

  • Natural look: Hair should look natural, not artificial or over-styled. No stiff hair-spray look.
  • Thickness and fullness: Consistent thickness root to tip — without looking too weighty
  • Shine and health: Bouncy, healthy appearance with a beautiful shine and reflective feel
  • Texture consistency: Use lighting to lift texture detail and highlight clean, detailed strands
  • Colour accuracy: Communicate the true colour as closely as possible

Product Imagery

Extension Product Shots

Clean, simple, and all about the hair. Beautifully styled extensions set against one of the vibrant primary colours.

Props: Flower

  • Same playful rules as people photography
  • Adds interest and complements the product — shouldn't steal the show
  • A little goes a long way

Props: Fluted/Reeded Glass

  • Hair can lay on it, over it, or beside it
  • The reflective quality mirrors the look of extensions: bright, light, almost translucent
  • Creates crisp, fresh tonal space with lovely shadows and reflections when lit with studio lights
  • Glass elevated above surface helps create shadows and reflections

Composition Approaches

Vertical glass

Glass placed vertically with hair extension draped across — light, reflection and refraction fall across the hair

Flatlay glass

Glass laid on coloured surface with hair draped across the glass — works well for larger products

Detail/close-up

Product laid across elevated glass — light passes through. Glass elevated above surface for shadows and reflections.

Hair flatlay

Hair dictates composition across bright colour background. Incorporate a flower to enhance the arrangement.

Haircare Product Shots

✓ Do

  • Use vibrant palette backgrounds
  • Use reeded glass prop for light/shadow/texture
  • Experiment with compositions — keep range looking fresh
  • Can incorporate a hair extension for context, to break up straight lines
  • Reeded glass flat lay with product, or glass vertical with product in front / behind

✗ Don't

  • Use flowers in haircare shots — could confuse customers by suggesting ingredients or fragrance

Neutral / Fallback Style

For scenarios requiring a stripped-back approach (e.g. third-party retailer requests): use off-white palette colour (#FFF2E0) as background, or a neutral/warm grey. This is not the preferred brand style.

Ecommerce (Ecomm)

Ecommerce content should use all the brand components covered in these guidelines, kept simple and uncluttered.

Composition Rule

Keep compositions simple and clear. Don't force too much information into one space. If something is worth covering, create a new tile for it.

Use Panelling

The panelling device is a key tool for ecomm. Gives a distinct, pragmatic way to contain imagery and information — ordered and accessible without being boring.

Content Types

Tiles can promote a product, highlight features, or bring attention to an outcome. Use the full brand colour palette and typography hierarchy.
